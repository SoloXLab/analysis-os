# Example: Regional EV Sales Decline

**Problem:** A specific regional market's EV sales dropped 15% quarter-over-quarter while the national EV market grew 8% in the same period.

**System Model highlights:** Regional sales as a function of (national demand trend) x (regional-specific factors: local incentives, charging infra density, competitor entries, dealer network changes).

**Observation:** Regional data shows a competitor opened 3 new dealerships in the region during the quarter; regional EV purchase incentive program expired mid-quarter and was not renewed at time of analysis; national trend data confirms the broader market grew.

**Hypothesis ranking:**
1. (High confidence) Incentive expiration — price-sensitive segment purchases are visibly concentrated in the weeks before the expiration date, then drop sharply after, consistent with a price/incentive-driven effect rather than a competitive one.
2. (Medium confidence) New competitor dealerships — too recent to have driven the full quarter's decline given typical sales-cycle lag, but likely a contributing factor going forward.

**Root cause:** Primarily the incentive expiration (a policy/pricing factor), not competitive pressure or weakening underlying regional demand — the national trend rules out a demand-side explanation.

**Prediction:** Absent incentive renewal, decline likely persists or worsens as the new competitor dealerships mature (Hypothesis 2 effect lags in).

**Optimization:** P0 — advocate for incentive renewal or a company-funded bridge incentive, quantifying expected sales recovery based on the pre/post-expiration purchase pattern already observed. P1 — competitive response plan for the new dealerships independent of the incentive question.

**Unknowns:** Whether the incentive program is renewable at all (policy decision outside company control) — analysis informs the ask but can't resolve this.
